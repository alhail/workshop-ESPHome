WEP 2025 Workshop: Smart Home Automation with ESPHome

Workshop Overview

Join us for a hands-on workshop on Smart Home Automation with ESPHome at WEP 2025. Over two days, you will learn how to set up and use ESPHome to create smart home projects. We'll cover how to connect sensors and devices, and how to make your home smarter with automation. This workshop is perfect for anyone interested in IoT and home automation, from beginners to tech enthusiasts. Come and discover new ideas and solutions for the future of smart homes.

Workshop Theme

Aligning with WEP 2025's theme of embracing exceptional individuals (outliers), this workshop focuses on empowering participants to become outliers in smart home technology by using ESPHome. It encourages innovative thinking and unconventional approaches to IoT and home automation.

Objectives

  • Highlighting Outliers: Showcase exceptional projects and individuals who have utilized ESPHome in innovative ways.
  • Hands-On Experience: Engage participants in practical exercises with ESPHome and IoT devices.
  • Foster Innovation: Encourage creative and unconventional approaches to smart home technology.

Course Outline

Day 1

  • Introduction to ESPHome
  • Setting Up ESPHome
  • Creating Your First ESPHome Project
  • Sensor and Actuator Integration

Day 2

  • Advanced ESPHome Configurations
  • Project: Smart Thermostat
  • Project: Home Security System
  • Troubleshooting and Optimization

Instructor

Mohamed Hail is an expert in IoT and smart home technologies, with extensive experience in using ESPHome for home automation projects. Mohamed Hail received his diploma and doctorate degrees in computer science from the University of Lübeck, Germany, in 2010 and 2018, respectively. In 2010, he joined the Center of Excellence CoSA (Communications - Systems - Applications) as a research assistant, focusing on communications, communications engineering, networks, distributed systems, and their applications. From 2013 to 2021, he worked as an embedded software developer for medical devices at Euroimmun AG, a PerkinElmer company. In 2018, he joined the Technical University of Applied Sciences Lübeck as an assistant lecturer in the Department of Electrical Engineering and Informatics. Since March 2021, he has been working as a Postdoctoral Researcher at the Institute of Telematics, with the goal of habilitation. Currently, he is the head of the IoT research group at the same institute. His research interests include the Internet of Things (IoT), Named Data Networking (NDN), artificial intelligence and machine learning, security, and future internet architecture. He also serves as the coordinator for the cooperation between the University of Lübeck and Sharjah University in the area of AI.
